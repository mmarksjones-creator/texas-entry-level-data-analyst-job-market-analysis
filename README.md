# Texas Entry-Level Data Analyst Job Market Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the Texas entry-level data analyst job market using a dataset of 100+ job postings. The goal was to identify which skills, cities, salary ranges, and experience requirements appear most often in entry-level analyst roles.

The project was designed to help answer one main business question:

**What skills and qualifications should an entry-level data analyst candidate focus on to become more competitive in the Texas job market?**

## Tools Used

- Excel
- SQL
- Power BI
- Python
- PivotTables
- KPI Dashboards
- Data Cleaning
- Data Visualization

## Business Problem

Entry-level analyst candidates often struggle to know which skills are most valuable when applying for jobs. Job postings can be overwhelming, inconsistent, and difficult to compare.

This analysis organizes real job posting data into clear insights showing the most requested skills, average salary ranges, top hiring cities, and typical experience expectations for entry-level analyst positions in Texas.

## Dataset

The dataset includes 100+ Texas entry-level data analyst job postings.

Key fields analyzed included:

- Job Title
- Company
- City
- Salary Range
- Required Skills
- Experience Requirement
- Excel Requirement
- SQL Requirement
- Power BI Requirement
- Python Requirement
- Remote/Hybrid/On-site Status

## Analysis Process

1. Collected 100+ entry-level analyst job postings in Texas.
2. Cleaned and organized the data in Excel.
3. Standardized salary ranges, job titles, city names, and skill categories.
4. Built PivotTables to summarize skill demand, salary trends, and location patterns.
5. Wrote SQL queries to analyze top skills, average salary, city demand, and experience requirements.
6. Built Excel and Power BI dashboards using KPI cards, charts, slicers, and filters.
7. Summarized findings into business recommendations for entry-level analyst candidates.

## Key Findings

- Excel appeared in approximately 62% of analyzed job postings.
- SQL appeared in approximately 61% of analyzed job postings.
- The average salary across analyzed postings was approximately $66,000.
- Austin appeared as one of the strongest Texas cities for entry-level analyst opportunities.
- Most entry-level postings preferred candidates with around 0–2 years of experience.
- Power BI, reporting, dashboards, and business communication appeared frequently in job descriptions.

## Dashboard Features

The dashboard includes:

- Total job postings analyzed
- Average salary
- Average experience requirement
- Top hiring cities
- Most requested technical skills
- Skill demand breakdown
- Salary trends by city
- Job posting distribution by location

## Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, entry-level analyst candidates in Texas should prioritize:

1. Excel for data cleaning, PivotTables, formulas, and dashboards.
2. SQL for querying, filtering, joining, and aggregating data.
3. Power BI for dashboard creation and business reporting.
4. Python for automation and data validation.
5. Portfolio projects that show business recommendations, not just charts.
6. Applications to Austin, Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, and other major Texas markets.

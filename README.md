# Texas Entry-Level Data Analyst Job Market Analysis

**Tools:** Excel · SQL · Power BI · Python

Entry-level analyst candidates in Texas often don't know which skills, cities, or experience levels actually matter when applying for jobs. This project analyzes 100+ real Texas job postings to answer that question with data — identifying the most requested skills, top hiring cities, salary ranges, and experience requirements so candidates can focus their preparation where it counts.

**Bottom line:** Excel and SQL appear in over 60% of postings. Austin leads all Texas cities in demand. Most roles require 0–2 years of experience, meaning the market is genuinely accessible to new graduates.

## Business recommendations

Based on this analysis, entry-level analyst candidates in Texas should:

1. Master Excel and SQL first — they appear in 6 out of 10 postings and are the baseline expectation for virtually every role
2. Add Power BI to stand out — BI tool proficiency shows up consistently in mid-to-upper entry-level postings
3. Target Austin, Dallas, and Houston — these three cities account for the majority of Texas demand
4. Don't wait on Python — it appears in only 26% of postings, making it a secondary priority behind the core tools
5. Apply broadly at the 0–2 year experience range — most postings are genuinely accessible without prior work experience

## Analysis process

1. Collected 100+ Texas entry-level data analyst job postings
2. Cleaned and standardized salary ranges, job titles, city names, and skill columns in Excel
3. Built PivotTables to summarize skill demand, salary trends, and city distribution
4. Wrote SQL queries to validate city demand, average salary, skill frequency, and experience requirements
5. Rebuilt the analysis in Power BI with KPI cards, slicers, and drill-down visuals
6. Translated findings into ranked business recommendations for candidates
